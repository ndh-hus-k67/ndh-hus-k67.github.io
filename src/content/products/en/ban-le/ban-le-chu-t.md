  content: |
    T hinges are commonly used for wooden doors, storage doors, garden gates, and other outdoor applications. The T-shaped design helps distribute weight more evenly across the door leaf, improving stability and load-bearing performance.
    </br></br>
    These hinges are typically manufactured from powder-coated steel, galvanized steel, or stainless steel (such as SS304 or SS316), providing durability and strong resistance to corrosion, especially in outdoor environments.
    </br></br>
    With a long strap and a shorter mounting plate, T hinges can be securely fixed to both the door frame and the door panel. This structure helps maintain smooth door movement while reducing the risk of door sagging over time.
    </br></br>
    Common technical specifications of T hinges include:
    </br></br>
    - Material: Stainless steel (SS304/SS316), galvanized steel, or powder-coated steel.
    </br>
    - Long strap length: Typically from 100 mm to 200 mm or more depending on the door size.
    </br>
    - Short leaf length: Usually from 50 mm to 100 mm depending on the hinge design.
    </br>
    - Material thickness: Approximately 2 mm to 5 mm for heavy-duty applications.
    </br>
    - Screw holes: The long strap usually includes multiple screw holes, while the short leaf typically has 3–4 holes for secure mounting.
    </br>
    - Accessories: Wood screws or metal screws (commonly M5×20 mm) depending on the installation surface.
    </br></br>
    T hinges are widely used for storage doors, garden gates, traditional wooden doors, truck cargo doors, and large wooden boxes where strong support and durable construction are required.
