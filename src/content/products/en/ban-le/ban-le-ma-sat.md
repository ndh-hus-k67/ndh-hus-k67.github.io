  content: |
    A friction stay hinge is a specialized window hinge that allows the window sash to remain open at different positions using friction between the hinge arms.
    </br></br>
    This type of hinge is commonly used for aluminum windows such as Xingfa systems, uPVC windows, and other casement or top-hung window designs in residential and commercial buildings.
    </br></br>
    The friction mechanism enables smooth operation while keeping the window stable at the desired opening position without requiring an additional stopper.
    </br></br>
    Typical technical specifications of friction stay hinges include:
    </br></br>
    - Material: SUS304 or SUS201 stainless steel for durability and corrosion resistance.
    </br>
    - Hinge length: typically from 10 inches to 18 inches (approximately 250 – 450 mm).
    </br>
    - Load capacity: about 20 – 40 kg per pair depending on the model.
    </br>
    - Opening angle: usually between 60° and 90° or more depending on the window design.
    </br>
    - Steel thickness: approximately 2.0 – 3.0 mm for improved rigidity and stability.
    </br></br>
    Friction stay hinges are widely used in aluminum window systems, uPVC window frames, and other casement or top-hung windows thanks to their ability to hold the window securely while providing smooth and quiet operation.
