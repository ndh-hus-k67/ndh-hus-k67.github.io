  content: |
    The adjustable toggle latch is an industrial fastening device designed to provide strong clamping force while allowing precise adjustment of the latch length. This feature makes it ideal for installations where surfaces may not be perfectly aligned or when high compression force is required.

    </br></br>
    Unlike standard toggle latches, this model includes a threaded adjustment bolt (U-bolt) that allows the hook length to be increased or shortened. By rotating the threaded bolt, users can easily fine-tune the tension between the latch body and the strike plate.

    </br></br>
    When the handle is pushed into the locked position, the toggle lever mechanism creates significant clamping force, ensuring that lids, covers or machine doors are tightly secured. This helps maintain sealing pressure on rubber gaskets and prevents dust, water or vibration from affecting the equipment.

    </br></br>
    Adjustable toggle latches are commonly manufactured from stainless steel or zinc plated steel to ensure durability in industrial environments. Stainless steel versions offer excellent corrosion resistance, while zinc plated steel provides a more economical option for general industrial use.

    </br></br>
    Thanks to their strong holding capacity and flexible adjustment capability, adjustable toggle latches are widely used in heavy machinery, transportation equipment, industrial containers and mechanical systems requiring tight sealing.

    </br></br>
    Typical technical specifications include:

    </br></br>
    - Material: Stainless steel 304 or zinc plated steel.
    </br>
    - Mechanism: Toggle lever with threaded adjustable hook.
    </br>
    - Adjustment range: Approximately 10 – 40 mm depending on size.
    </br>
    - Installation: Surface mounting with screws or bolts.
    </br>
    - Applications: Industrial machinery, containers, equipment enclosures and transport boxes.
